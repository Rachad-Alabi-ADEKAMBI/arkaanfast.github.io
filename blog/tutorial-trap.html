Tutorial Trap!

The reason I am writing a blog on this is because I had fallen into this trap once, and like me, many of my friends did too. So I decided to give you guys a heads up on this, about what it is (you must have understood from the title), and how not to fall for it.

When I began my journey as a developer, I was quite intrigued with the vastness of Computer Science and the innumerable opportunities that it had. I was quite confused, ‘What do I learn ??’, Web Development, App Development, AI, Cloud Computing etc, and in addition to these, so many programming languages.Let's look at what this Tutorial trap is, and then on how to get away from it..

First and foremost the most important thing is passion, do something that excites you, that fascinates you, so that doing it won't lead to boredom and you will be consistent with your work. If programming is not something that you are passionate about, keep exploring until you find what drives you, keep experimenting, keep failing, it does not matter because at the end of the day we ought to be proud and happy with ourselves, that's what matters !!.

As the name suggests, Tutorial trap is getting trapped in this never ending loop of jumping from one tutorial to the other. You might wonder, Why is it a trap ? Isn't it a good thing that we are doing courses and learning new things every time ? Well, from my experience I must say that it’s really frustrating and exhausting and definitely not at all a good idea. This way you will not see any progress in developing your career, at least I didn’t while I was trapped here and neither did my friends (developer buddies).

You think about it .…. you keep learning, learning and learning, but never really implement whatever you've learnt. You're not typing any code, not coming across bugs, not testing, and not failing, then how will you learn? It's a proven fact that only when you implement the things you learn, you improve and understand it better. I have experienced it as well and I am sure some of the pros reading this have experienced the same. If you have the knowledge, but have no skill implementing it then you have wasted your time acquiring it. If you are in this trap where you are constantly learning new programming languages one after the other, or learning new tech stacks one after the other, please stop doing it. You will get nowhere and it's going to take a toll on your head.

I have been reading this amazing book ‘The 7 Habits of highly effective people’ by Stephen R. Covey (I suggest you all give it a read). In this the author talks about something known as P/PC balance. I will just give a brief of what it is and if you want more insight on it, do check the book out. So here, in P/PC, the P stands for Production and PC stands for Production Capability. Production is the outcome of Production Capability. Production Capability is what you learn through courses/tutorials and Production is how you develop something out of what you have learnt. The balance here is very important and if not maintained, could lead to negativity or you might even consider quitting.

How to get rid of it ? ....it’s pretty simple. In terms of learning a new language, let’s say you have learnt Python as your first language, don't jump into something else after you have got your basics right (please read my previous blog). Use your Python knowledge and build some simple projects using it. Try solving coding problems using Python.This way you will be typing, coming across a lot of bugs (and bugs are really good to come across too, you learn and get to know so many different things) I love bugs, to be honest!!.
