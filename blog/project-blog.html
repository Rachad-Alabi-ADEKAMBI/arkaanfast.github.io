Projects !!

As mentioned in the previous blogs, it is important that you start building projects while you are learning, this way you learn more and grow faster. In this blog I will be addressing a common problem you might face while working on your first project. Also as a bonus I will give you the link to my first ever project I had developed after I learnt python. It's nothing fancy, just a simple project.

I know it's been so long since my last blog here. Before going ahead make sure you have read the previous blogs. So let's get started, shall we ?

Hold on, I need to address one more very important thing now. Guys, there will be times you get frustrated, you get stuck and feel like there is no way to solve it. This happens even to most of the experienced developers, and it is completely normal. The important thing is that you must NOT GIVE UP. Whenever you come across any problems or bugs, remember there is always a way to solve it. If you don't know how to, you will eventually find out, just take a few deep breaths, get your mind off the task, relax and try again. Trust me, 90% of the time this works. Even if you can’t find the solution, we have the internet to our rescue !! So just google your problems out and you will come across a solution, but do remember when you do this it is very important to understand the solution, go through each line of the code and understand how it is working. This is VERY IMPORTANT as it will help you improve and you could tell yourself that “Now I am a better developer than who I was yesterday “. Also, you can reach out to an experienced coder who can help you (developer buddies remember), surround yourself with people you think are better at coding than you, so that you can learn and grow. If you don't have a developer buddyI am always there to help you out !

Whenever you are working on something, make it simple, and this is something I got from a lot of self development books that I’ve read. Make it simple first then make it efficient and add all the features you need. Earlier when I had started developing projects, I always used to think of the complexities, the advanced stuff and I kept on reading articles, watching videos etc. Do not do this. Doing this would waste your time and also you might not start the project, this has happened to me multiple times. That's when a senior of mine told me not to think too much and just start with your project and MAKE IT SIMPLE then go for efficiency. This is a better and a faster approach, which helped me grow as a developer. You might be wondering, how is it fast? Won't it be faster if we think of an efficient solution first? Yes! It would be faster if you can think of an efficient solution first, but most of the time that usually does not happen if you are a beginner, although with practice you will get better and faster at it. So make it simple, JUST START and be in the process.

Let me share my experience while I was building this snake and ladders game. The game I had developed was nothing fancy, no GUI, just a terminal input-output kinda thing (run the code and you will find out!). It was just giving input and checking the output kinda game,where you can choose the number of players playing, give player names and the game starts. Hitting enter, you see the number on the terminal and then you move up the board if you encounter a snake or a ladder, it pops up a message on the terminal. The first time I developed this project, it was not good at all, well I thought it was awesome but when I had asked my senior Ihthisham to review it he told me “BRO THIS IS NOT GOOD”, and then I asked him, why? It's working fine. He said yes good work with that but try to make it efficient now, its over 300 lines of code you can improve it. So I invested some more time on this project, learnt a few new concepts (classes and objects and how to use it effectively) and reduced the 300 lines of code to just around 59! Trust me one of the best feelings ever!

So guys do start working and hustling and remember you will be coming across bugs, it just means that it is working and you are improving, you learn from the bugs, solve it and you grow and learn more. So keep at it and DON'T GIVE UP.
